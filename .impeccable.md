# Kerno Design Context

## Users and jobs

Kerno serves developers using Codex in medium or large, long-lived repositories. They need to understand unfamiliar code quickly, avoid stale assumptions, inspect exactly why context was selected, and verify that model-routing claims reflect runtime evidence.

## Brand and personality

Kerno feels like an industrial editorial evidence console: precise, candid, calm under pressure, and quietly distinctive. It should feel closer to a well-designed lab instrument and engineering notebook than a generic AI dashboard.

## Visual language

- Light-first warm paper neutrals with near-black ink typography.
- Signal orange marks active reasoning and context expansion; verified green marks evidence-backed success; muted red marks stale or failed evidence.
- Editorial serif display type paired with a compact technical sans and monospace numerals.
- Fine rules, generous negative space, asymmetric composition, and dense information only where the user asks for detail.
- Motion is restrained and respects reduced-motion preferences.
- Avoid cyan-purple gradients, glowing dark cards, excessive pills, dashboard-card grids, and decorative AI imagery.

## Design principles

1. Truth is visible: recorded, estimated, recommended, requested, and observed states are never conflated.
2. Correctness comes first: tests and review outcomes precede token or latency metrics.
3. Progressive disclosure mirrors progressive context expansion.
4. Every important number links back to evidence.
5. Color reinforces labels; it never carries meaning alone.
6. The primary task timeline remains understandable at 1440×900 without narration and usable at 1024px.
