## Design Context

### Users

Kerno serves developers using Codex on unfamiliar, medium-to-large, long-lived repositories and hackathon judges evaluating the product in a short, evidence-focused session. Their job is to understand whether Codex received the smallest trustworthy context, why it received it, what changed after runtime evidence, which model was actually requested or observed, and whether the result is correct.

### Brand Personality

Precise, candid, and editorial. Kerno should create confidence through provenance and restraint, feel technically serious without looking like a generic observability console, and make uncertainty as legible as success.

### Aesthetic Direction

Use Kerno's warm engineered editorial system: ivory canvas, soft-white surfaces, warm graphite structure, oxide orange for primary action/context, and aubergine only for reviewer/secondary evidence. Use a serious neo-grotesk for the wordmark and display hierarchy plus compact monospace evidence details. Dark mode is warm graphite, never navy. Avoid blue, cyan, teal, mint, generic green branding, sci-fi glow, decorative gradients, glassmorphism, generic card grids, fake graphs, and unlabelled animation. Replay, live, measured, estimated, experimental, unavailable, success, and error states must remain visually and textually distinct.

### Design Principles

1. Lead with the proof loop: initial context, contradictory evidence, targeted expansion, verified result, invalidation.
2. Never turn missing evidence into a reassuring visual; label truth states in words as well as color.
3. Reveal depth progressively: judges understand the outcome immediately and can inspect provenance, scores, routes, and raw evidence on demand.
4. Prefer structured editorial hierarchy and purposeful whitespace over dashboards made from interchangeable cards.
5. Preserve keyboard access, readable contrast, reduced motion, responsive layouts, and honest empty/error states.
6. Consume semantic tokens from `@kerno/brand`; never add raw component colors or hand-edit generated brand mirrors.
